An exuberantly beautiful city with a joyous soul. Rio de Janeiro is an endless parade of attractions, driven by its principal asset, the ‘Cariocas’, as the residents of Rio are known. From the top of Corcovado Mountain, where the Statue of Christ gives his open-armed blessing to the city, one can seethe sea, the mountains, the forest and the lake ... a truly incomparable setting. One of the richest stretches of Atlantic coastline is trimmed with famous beaches,such as Copacabana and Ipanema, peopled by sun worshippers. The tastes of Rio are everywhere, in the kiosks, bars and restaurants, drenched in sun or glowing in the moonlight. This is Rio, where everyone, from near or far, feels at home in this contagiously effervescent environment

Each samba school has 80 minutes to perform and show off their hard effort of having worked during the past 365 days! Each school has a ‘theme’ and all costumes are accordingly. They have about 3500 to 5000 performers, all of them dressed in rich costumes that describe the plot of the story the school is presenting. The subjects of these stories are usually related to either famous world personalities or historical aspects of Brazil. The main parts of the theme are shown on allegorical cars, that vary in number (from 5 to 8) and in size (from 8 to 15 meters high). The song’s lyrics make it easier the understanding of what goes on and the more the audience accepts their beat and music, the more they will be cheered and win points. In the past there were tribunes all over town where the parades used to take place. But it became too expensive and that’s why today we have the Sambodrome, specially built for these parades. They all take place there and it is a copy of a large avenue, 1.5km long and they have 01 hour to get through it. If no, they loose points. The schools are actually all similar in quality and there are the leading ones, which means there have been contest going on, and only the 8 winners parade for the champion ship. Sugarloaf Mountain is a peak situated in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, at the mouth of Guanabara Bay on a peninsula that juts out into the Atlantic Ocean. Rising 396 m above the harbor, the peak is named for its resemblance to the traditional shape of concentrated refined loaf sugar.

Christ the Redeemer is an Art Deco statue of Jesus Christ in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, created by French sculptor Paul Landowski and built by Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa, in collaboration with French engineer Albert Caquot. Romanian sculptor Gheorghe Leonida sculpted the face
